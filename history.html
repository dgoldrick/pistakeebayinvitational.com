The Beginning

How It All Started

Sometime in the 1950s, on the shores of Pistakee Bay in McHenry, Illinois, a group of men decided to make a golf outing into something more. What began as an informal gathering became, over time, one of the most enduring traditions in the family — an annual tournament played every summer, without fail, for over seventy years.

The early years belong to the founding generation: Bill Crawford, Tom Goldrick, Joe Goldrick, Tim McCabe, Gary Micetich, Bill McCabe, John Goldrick, Mike Goldrick, and others who first teed it up together at Pistakee Bay Country Club on West Bay Road. The club sat at the edge of the bay, a classic nine-hole layout that would host the tournament for decades.

The format was simple then and remains largely the same today: four nine-hole scrambles spread across Friday, and one eighteen-hole stroke play round to crown the champion. The stroke play scores became the basis for handicaps the following year — your past performance set your starting point, a built-in mechanism that kept the competition honest and gave everyone a fighting chance.

Every year we come back — older, a little slower off the tee, and absolutely certain that this is the year we finally win the thing.

— A Tournament Competitor, sentiments shared widely

Pistakee Bay Country Club has since closed — the property sold, the course gone. But the tournament carries its name and its spirit forward. The men who started it taught their sons to play. Those sons brought their own sons. Today, three generations of Goldricks, McCabes, Pierces, and their friends gather each June to continue what the founding generation started.

The scorecard has changed. The players have changed. The stories told at the 19th hole have gotten longer and more embellished with each passing year. But the tournament endures.

For the first time in the tournament's history, the event is cancelled. COVID-19 brings a seven-decade streak to a pause. No champion is crowned.

The tournament resumes. Bill Crawford — a name from the very first scorecards — wins the trophy. A fitting champion for a comeback year.
2022 – 2024

Patrick McCabe wins in 2022. Mike Goldrick in 2023. Dan Goldrick and TJ McCabe share the honors in 2024.

The Story Continues
Twenty-three players. Three generations. One trophy. The Pistakee Bay Invitational plays on.
How We Play

The Tournament Format

Every year the format is the same: 54 holes played over a single day. Four nine-hole scrambles in the morning and afternoon, followed by the round that matters — eighteen holes of stroke play.

The handicap system is simple and earned: your handicap for the current year is calculated from the average of your two prior years' gross scores, minus par. Play better, carry less strokes. Play worse, get more help. Every score you post becomes part of your permanent record.
